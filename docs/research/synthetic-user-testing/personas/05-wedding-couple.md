# Persona 5: Maya Okafor & Daniel Reyes — Bride & Groom Planning Wedding Weekend

Code-grounded simulation, compiled 2026-06-09. Primary source:
`docs/research/synthetic-user-testing/evidence/product-ground-truth.md`. Every finding labeled
`[OBSERVED — cite]`, `[SIMULATED RISK]`, or `[HYPOTHESIS — needs live test]`.

## A. Profile

- **Maya Okafor (31)** — product marketing manager. High tech comfort; lives in Notion, Google Sheets, Partiful. Owns the planning spreadsheet. **Daniel Reyes (33)** — high-school teacher. Medium tech comfort; will use whatever Maya picks, but is the one fielding "how do I open the link?" calls from his parents.
- **The event:** 3-day wedding weekend in Santa Barbara — rehearsal dinner (Fri, 30 people), ceremony + reception (Sat, ~120 guests), afterparty (Sat night, ~60), farewell brunch (Sun, ~80). Hotel block at two properties, shuttles between hotel ↔ venue.
- **Guest range:** ages 22–75. Wedding party (10) needs private logistics (hair/makeup times, lineup order, vendor contacts). All guests need a clean public schedule. Daniel's 68-year-old mother and Maya's 75-year-old aunt are mobile-only and struggle with account creation.
- **Planning style:** Maya: maximal, desktop, 11 months out. Daniel: delegated tasks only.
- **Pain points today:** Zola website is pretty but static (no chat, no live updates); the family group chats (3 of them) are chaos; photo collection after the wedding is a mess of AirDrop and "can you text me that one."
- **Budget sensitivity:** Paradoxical. They're spending $60k on the wedding, so $20–100 is noise — **but** they're nickeled-and-dimed by every vendor and resent recurring subscriptions for a one-time event. A one-off pass framed as "for your wedding weekend" is psychologically easy; a $19.99/mo subscription is not, and a $99/mo "Growth Pro" business tier is insulting.
- **Why adopt:** one link that replaces (wedding website schedule page + group chat + shared album + shuttle-time blast texts), with broadcasts for day-of changes.
- **Why reject:** any guest-facing friction (mandatory account creation for a 75-year-old), any unprofessional or off-tone copy in front of 120 guests, any ambiguity about who sees the wedding-party-only logistics, or pricing that reads like it was designed for concert promoters.

## B. Jobs-to-be-Done

1. When I am **two months out from the wedding**, I want to **publish one mobile-friendly weekend schedule (rehearsal → ceremony → reception → afterparty → brunch) with addresses and shuttle times**, so I can **stop answering the same "what time / where do I park" texts**.
2. When I am **coordinating the wedding party**, I want to **keep hair/makeup call times, lineup order, and vendor contacts visible only to the 10 wedding-party members**, so I can **avoid 120 guests seeing (or being notified about) internal logistics**.
3. When I am **inviting 120 guests across ages 22–75**, I want **a join flow my aunt can complete in under a minute on her phone**, so I can **get >90% of guests actually inside the app instead of half of them texting me**.
4. When **the shuttle time changes on Saturday morning**, I want to **send one broadcast that visibly reaches every guest**, so I can **be confident nobody is stranded at the hotel**.
5. When **the weekend is over**, I want **every guest's phone photos pooled in one place**, so I can **collect candids the photographer missed without chasing 120 people**.

## C. Full User Journey (16 steps)

### 1. Discovery / landing
- **Tried:** Maya googles group-event tools, lands on the marketing page.
- **What code says:** Unauthed visitors get `src/MarketingApp.tsx` / `src/components/landing/FullPageLanding.tsx` with pricing, FAQ, sign-up modal; SEO landing pages are travel-themed (`/trip-planner`, `/group-trip-planner`) [OBSERVED — ground-truth §3, §2]. Pricing copy: "Start free. Upgrade when your trip gets serious." [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7, `PricingSection.tsx`].
- **Friction:** Everything says *trip*. Nothing on the landing surface speaks to weddings; the closest Pro category is "Celebrations" buried inside Pro trip creation [OBSERVED — `CreateTripModal` categories, ground-truth §4]. [SIMULATED RISK] Maya only finds Chravel if a friend used it; she would never find it searching "wedding weekend app."
- **Verdict:** Usable, not aimed at her. She arrives skeptical.

### 2. Sign-up / onboarding
- **Tried:** Maya signs up with Google on desktop.
- **What code says:** Email+password, Google OAuth, Apple Sign-in; email verification required; 9-screen skippable onboarding carousel (Welcome → Chat → Calendar → … → Concierge) [OBSERVED — ground-truth §3].
- **Friction:** Fine for Maya. The same flow is what *every guest* must later complete (see step 4) — that's where it bites. [SIMULATED RISK] Onboarding screens are trip-framed; nothing confirms "yes, this works for a wedding."
- **Verdict:** Smooth for the couple.

### 3. Trip/Event creation — consumer trip vs Event
- **Tried both paths.**
- **Consumer trip path:** default type, free, title/dates/timezone required, cover photo with crop modal [OBSERVED — ground-truth §4]. Works immediately, but a consumer trip has no RSVP, no attendee model, no broadcasts, no agenda tab — 120 full-write members in one chat. [SIMULATED RISK] Chaos at 120 people; consumer path is only right for the 10-person wedding party.
- **Event path — is it paywalled immediately? NO.** Despite ground-truth §4's summary ("Event creation gated to Frequent Chraveler"), the billing source of truth gives **Free and Explorer 3 lifetime events**: `event_creation: { free: 3, explorer: 3, 'frequent-chraveler': -1 }` with the comment "Events are a Frequent Chraveler benefit. Free + Explorer get 3 events total (lifetime)" [OBSERVED — `src/billing/entitlements.ts:296-302`]. The modal renders an "Event" toggle for everyone, asks for "Event Title," timezone ("Helps attendees from other time zones know when events occur"), and an Organizer field [OBSERVED — `src/components/CreateTripModal.tsx:403-657`].
- **Friction A (copy tone):** Organizer field help text — "The organization, company, or group hosting this event" [OBSERVED — `CreateTripModal.tsx:531`]. Maya types "Maya & Daniel" into a field written for corporations. [SIMULATED RISK] First "this isn't for weddings" signal.
- **Friction B (wrong upsell):** If they were on their 4th lifetime event, the `UPGRADE_REQUIRED_EVENT` error fires a toast built from `trip_cap_event` paywall config whose copy is "Run unlimited Events and keep every production workspace active." with `recommendedPlan: 'Explorer'` [OBSERVED — `src/components/subscription/featurePaywall.ts:96-101`, `CreateTripModal.tsx:321-324`]. **Two defects:** (a) "production workspace" is touring-crew language shown to a bride; (b) Explorer does *not* lift the event cap — Explorer is also 3 lifetime; only Frequent Chraveler does [OBSERVED — `entitlements.ts:300-302` vs `featurePaywall.ts:99`]. The paywall recommends a plan that doesn't fix the problem.
- **Verdict:** Event creation itself works free of charge — good. But it's dressed in B2B clothes, and the upsell path is factually wrong.

### 4. Inviting guest types — wedding party vs family vs all guests
- **Tried:** Maya wants three audiences: wedding party (full access + private logistics), parents/VIPs, all guests (read-mostly).
- **What code says:** Events have exactly **two roles**: `event_organizer` (full `*` access) and `event_attendee` [OBSERVED — `src/types/permissionMatrix.generated.ts:124-163`]; role resolution is binary — `tripType === 'event' ? (isOrganizer ? 'event_organizer' : 'event_attendee')` [OBSERVED — `src/hooks/useMutationPermissions.ts:78`], and organizer = creator/admin or a member role literally named "organizer"/"admin" [OBSERVED — `src/hooks/useEventPermissions.ts:54-61`]. Role-based channels and the roles/roster system are **Pro Starter ($49/mo)+ entitlements** (`channels_enabled`, `roles_enabled`) — not on any consumer tier [OBSERVED — `src/billing/config.ts:105-117`]. Invite links are trip-wide (`/join/{token}`, optional expiry/max-uses/approval) with no per-role invite [OBSERVED — ground-truth §5].
- **Friction:** **The wedding's core information-architecture need — wedding party ≠ all guests — cannot be modeled inside one Event.** Workaround: a separate free consumer trip for the wedding party + the Event for everyone. Two spaces, two chats, double posting. [SIMULATED RISK] Maya will run the workaround; Daniel will post the shuttle change in the wrong one.
- **Older-guest auth friction:** Invitee sees a nice preview ("Trip starts in X days", member count) but then hits a hard sign-in/create-account gate before joining [OBSERVED — ground-truth §5; `src/pages/JoinTrip.tsx` renders "Request to Join" only post-auth]. Preview copy reads "12 **Chravelers already planning**" and the share unfurl says "📍 Santa Barbara • You've been invited to join a trip! Click to see details and join the adventure." [OBSERVED — `JoinTrip.tsx:878, 232-243`]. For a 75-year-old aunt invited to a *wedding*, "join the adventure" + mandatory account + email verification is a real drop-off cliff. [HYPOTHESIS — needs live test] Guest-join completion for 60+ guests well under 70%; no telemetry exists to check (PostHog has ingested zero events — ground-truth §9).
- **Post-approval bug:** if approval mode is on, there's a known issue where an approved member's trip is **missing from their dashboard** [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10.6, DEBUG_PATTERNS.md]. For a wedding that's "Aunt Carol says the app is broken."
- **Verdict:** Invite mechanics are solid for one audience; the wedding needs three. Fails JTBD #2 structurally.

### 5. Adding rehearsal dinner / ceremony / reception / afterparty / brunch / hotel block / transportation
- **What code says:** Event tabs are Admin, **Agenda (always on)**, Calendar, Chat, **Line-up (always on)**, Media, Polls, Tasks [OBSERVED — `src/lib/eventTabs.ts:24-32`]. Calendar supports event creation with place autocomplete; Smart Import can ingest PDFs/ICS/emails [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6]. Multiple basecamps exist (`src/components/places/BasecampsPanel.tsx` referencing accommodations types) so each hotel can be a saved base [OBSERVED — file present; depth of "hotel block" support is thin]. There is **no lodging/room-block or shuttle primitive** for events — room assignments and logistics live in Pro Growth's partially-stubbed logistics module [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6 "per-diem/compliance/settlement partially stubbed", `logistics_management` is Growth-only in `config.ts:140`].
- **Friction:** The five functions become calendar entries + saved places — workable. The hotel block becomes "a place plus a chat message with the booking code" and shuttles become calendar entries; neither is structured. **"Line-up" is an always-on tab that cannot be disabled** [OBSERVED — `eventTabs.ts:29` `alwaysOn: true`] — concert vocabulary permanently in a wedding's tab rail. [SIMULATED RISK] Guests ask what "Line-up" means; Maya can't remove it, only ignore it.
- **Organizer tab control is good:** optional tabs can be toggled; disabled tabs show a lock and aria-label "(disabled by organizer)" with a `DisabledTabDialog` [OBSERVED — `EventDetailContent.tsx:218-228`, `src/components/events/DisabledTabDialog.tsx`]. The legacy bug where NULL `enabled_features` made all tabs look "disabled by admin" is **fixed** (NULL now normalizes to legacy defaults) — cite as regression-watch only [OBSERVED — DEBUG_PATTERNS.md:280-299].
- **Verdict:** Multi-day schedule: yes. Hotel block and shuttles: improvised, not modeled.

### 6. AI Concierge
- **What code says:** 38 tools / 18 query classes; writes go through a "Save to Trip?" pending-action confirm card. Free tier = **10 queries/user/trip** [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6, §7]. Voice concierge is architecturally broken (edge functions can't hold WebSockets; "Voice setup timed out") and is the marquee Frequent Chraveler feature [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10.2, AUDIT_CONCIERGE_LIVE.md].
- **Friction:** Useful to Maya for restaurant recs near the venue; 10 free queries is enough to taste, not to rely on. [SIMULATED RISK] If she upgrades partly for voice and it times out, that's a refund-request moment at wedding price sensitivity. Design friction: concierge "Action Plan JSON" mandate frequently ignored; preference injection on irrelevant queries [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 design frictions].
- **Verdict:** Nice-to-have; not a wedding driver; voice must not be sold to her.

### 7. Places / Basecamp
- **What code says:** Google Places search/save, basecamp as home base feeding distances and concierge context, map + list views [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6; `BasecampSelector.tsx`, `BasecampsPanel.tsx`].
- **Friction:** Genuinely good for "venue, two hotels, brunch spot, parking." `event_attendee` has basecamp/links **read-only** [OBSERVED — `permissionMatrix.generated.ts:151-162`] — correct for a wedding (guests can't edit the venue pin).
- **Verdict:** One of the strongest fits. Beats a static wedding-website map.

### 8. Polls
- **What code says:** Two permission layers disagree on attendees. The generated matrix gives `event_attendee` polls `write: false` [OBSERVED — `permissionMatrix.generated.ts:139-144`], while `useEventPermissions` grants `polls: { canVote: true, canCreate: isOrganizer }` ("All members can vote") [OBSERVED — `useEventPermissions.ts:97-101`]. Intended behavior: attendees vote, only organizers create — exactly right for "Song requests?" / "Brunch: 10 or 11?". Anonymous option, realtime counts exist [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6].
- **Friction:** [SIMULATED RISK] The matrix-vs-hook drift is a latent break: any code path that gates *voting* on the generated matrix instead of `useEventPermissions` silently blocks 120 guests from voting. No observed bug today, but two sources of truth for the same question is how the next regression ships.
- **Verdict:** Works; flag the drift.

### 9. Tasks
- **What code says:** Multi-assignment, due dates, mine/unassigned/overdue filters [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6]. Event attendees are read-only on tasks via both layers; `EventTasksTab` receives `permissions=eventPermissions.tasks` [OBSERVED — `EventDetailContent.tsx:189`, `permissionMatrix.generated.ts:133-138`].
- **Friction:** Tasks are organizer-only to write — fine for "Daniel: confirm shuttle count." But the wedding party (groomsmen picking up the arch) are *attendees* in the Event, so they can't be assigned actionable tasks there — pushing the couple back to the separate wedding-party trip workaround (step 4).
- **Verdict:** Adequate for the couple, blocked for helpers inside the Event.

### 10. Payments
- **What code says:** Log expense, equal/custom split, settle-up via manual or **Venmo deeplink — no in-app money movement**; free tier = 3 splits/trip [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6, §7]. Known critical: settlement double-credit race, missing idempotency keys [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10.1].
- **Friction:** Weddings barely need guest payments (maybe afterparty bar tab, bachelor-party leftovers). The wedding party splitting the party bus is a real but minor use. [SIMULATED RISK] The double-credit race is exactly the kind of money bug that, if it hits a family member, permanently torches trust at a wedding.
- **Verdict:** Irrelevant to most of the weekend; the open race is a reputational landmine.

### 11. Media — guest photo sharing (strongest use case?)
- **What code says:** Photos/videos/links/files; grid + lightbox; compression pipeline; iOS share-sheet ingestion [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6, `src/components/UnifiedMediaHub.tsx`]. Event attendees explicitly **can upload** — code comment: "All members can upload (encouraged for photo sharing)"; organizers can moderate/delete any [OBSERVED — `useEventPermissions.ts:85-90`]. Free storage = 500 MB; quotas are **advisory-only** and the media bucket is not signed-URL enforced [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7, §10.7]. Recently-fixed fragility: "Unable to preview" tiles for chat uploads, cover-photo pipeline drift [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 recently-fixed].
- **Friction:** This IS the killer wedding feature — 120 phones pooling candids beats every wedding-website photo widget and beats a shared iCloud album (which excludes Android). But: 120 guests × wedding video clips blows through 500 MB in the first hour; since quotas are advisory-only, it *works* but the couple is silently out of plan, and a later enforcement change could strand their wedding photos behind a paywall. [SIMULATED RISK] Also, unmoderated upload from 120 people includes the drunk-afterparty problem; organizer delete exists, but there's no review queue.
- **Verdict:** Strongest single reason to adopt. Storage honesty is the catch.

### 12. Chat
- **What code says:** Stream Chat with threads, reactions, pins, mentions; Events render `<TripChat enableGroupChat showBroadcasts isEvent />` [OBSERVED — `EventDetailContent.tsx:172`]. All attendees can send [OBSERVED — `useEventPermissions.ts:81-83`]. Recently-fixed: message loss on reconnect, mobile horizontal overflow stealing tab taps [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 recently-fixed].
- **Friction:** One chat for 120 guests, no sub-channels at consumer/event tier (channels are Pro Starter+). [SIMULATED RISK] The family group-chat chaos Chravel promises to fix gets *recreated* at 120 people in a single room; the couple can disable the Chat tab entirely (it's optional [OBSERVED — `eventTabs.ts:28`]), which many will — sacrificing the social layer to protect signal.
- **Verdict:** Either noisy or off. Broadcasts (step 13) are the real channel.

### 13. Notifications / broadcasts
- **What code says:** Broadcasts are first-class on events with RSVP responses stored via Supabase, "RSVP & New Attendees" notification category, and an `EmergencyBroadcast` component [OBSERVED — `src/hooks/stream/useStreamBroadcasts.ts:9,35-36`, `src/components/events/EmergencyBroadcast.tsx`]. Known: **notification fanout runs synchronously in the INSERT transaction** (scales badly), **no per-trip mute, no batching** [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10.4, NOTIFICATION_AUDIT.md].
- **Friction:** "Shuttle now leaves 4:15" broadcast with delivery tracking is exactly JTBD #4 — genuinely better than a blast text. But with no per-trip mute, guests who find the chat noisy will disable Chravel notifications entirely at the OS level — and then miss the shuttle broadcast. The fanout-blocking issue is rated against 4,000-member events; 120 guests is likely fine [HYPOTHESIS — needs live test].
- **Verdict:** Best-in-class concept; the no-mute gap actively undermines its reliability.

### 14. The weekend itinerary as a GUEST sees it
- **What code says:** Mobile guests land on `MobileEventDetail` / `EventDetailContent` with a tab rail; default tab is `agenda` [OBSERVED — `src/pages/EventDetail.tsx:48`, `EventDetailContent` tab map]. Agenda is always-on; attendees get read-only calendar/agenda, can chat/upload/vote. Desktop event view auto-scrolls to chat in demo mode [OBSERVED — `EventDetail.tsx:104-119`].
- **Friction:** The guest view is an *app workspace*, not an *invitation*. Dark premium/gold theme [OBSERVED — `JoinTrip.tsx` gold-gradient styling] is handsome but generic — no couple's names/monogram/photo-first hero the way Zola/Joy present. The aunt sees: tab bar with "Admin"-adjacent vocabulary, "Line-up," "Basecamp." [SIMULATED RISK] Guests experience it as "the couple made us download a logistics app," not "the couple shared their wedding."
- **Verdict:** Functionally complete, emotionally flat for the occasion.

### 15. Returning later
- **What code says:** Event must appear in the guest's trips list to resolve (`userTrips.find(t => t.id === eventId && t.trip_type === 'event')`, else not-found) [OBSERVED — `EventDetail.tsx:71-76`]; with the known approved-member-missing-from-dashboard issue [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10.6], a guest who joined via approval may return a week later and not see the wedding at all. Free tier allows only **3 active trips** — guests with other trips may bump into the cap [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7] (whether joined trips count against it: [HYPOTHESIS — needs live test]).
- **Friction:** Post-wedding, the Event becomes the photo archive — strong retention hook — but only if storage stays accessible past advisory quota (step 11).
- **Verdict:** Return path exists; the dashboard-missing bug is the scariest single defect for this persona.

### 16. Pay-or-upgrade decision
- **What code says:** Wedding Event #1 is free (3 lifetime). Caps: Frequent Chraveler $19.99/mo → `events_attendees_100`; Growth Pro $99/mo → `events_attendees_200`; an `events_attendees_50` entitlement exists in types but is assigned to no tier [OBSERVED — `config.ts:87,142,168`, `types.ts:40-43`]. **Crucially, the attendee cap is not enforced anywhere:** `events_attendees_*` appears only in billing config/types/entitlements with zero call sites in `src/` or `supabase/functions/`; `join-trip` declares a `TRIP_FULL` error code but never produces it (only invite `max_uses` blocks joins) [OBSERVED — grep of repo; `supabase/functions/join-trip/index.ts:36,193-202`]. Trip Passes: Explorer 45-day $39.99, Frequent Chraveler 90-day $74.99 [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7, `config.ts` TRIP_PASS_PRODUCTS]. Growth Pro has **no self-serve checkout — Pro CTAs are mailto: links** [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7].
- **The collision, resolved:** A 120-guest wedding nominally exceeds the 100-attendee Frequent Chraveler cap, and the only published tier covering 120 is a $99/mo B2B plan reachable via *email to sales*. In practice all 120 guests will join unblocked because nothing enforces the cap. So the couple is either (a) honest readers of the pricing page who conclude "Chravel can't host our wedding without a business plan" and bounce, or (b) accidental cap violators on free tier. Both outcomes are bad: (a) loses the sale, (b) loses the revenue and sets up a future enforcement betrayal.
- **What they'd actually pay:** $74.99 Frequent Chraveler 90-day Trip Pass covers the planning runway + weekend + photo collection, with unlimited storage and unlimited AI — *if* its 100-attendee implication didn't scare them. They would never subscribe monthly for a one-time event, and never email sales for $99/mo.
- **Verdict:** The natural product ($75 one-off pass) exists; the attendee-cap pricing copy actively talks a 120-guest wedding out of buying it.

## E. Emotional Reaction

- **Confident:** Places/basecamps, agenda building, broadcast concept, guest photo upload. Maya: "This is genuinely better than my spreadsheet + 3 group chats."
- **Confused:** "Line-up"? "Production workspace"? Why does the upsell recommend Explorer when the table says Explorer also gets 3 events? Which plan actually allows 120 guests?
- **Annoyed:** Every guest needs an account with email verification; no way to give the wedding party a private space without a second workspace or a $49/mo business plan; travel-bro invite copy ("join the adventure," "Chravelers") in front of grandparents.
- **Impressed:** The join preview page is genuinely polished (cover photo, countdown, member count) [OBSERVED — JoinTrip.tsx render]; pending-action confirm cards on AI writes feel trustworthy.
- **Would abandon when:** the first elderly guest fails to join, or when they read "100 attendees" on the Frequent Chraveler column and conclude their 120-guest wedding needs a $99/mo sales call. [SIMULATED RISK] Abandonment happens at *pricing-page reading time*, before any bug is ever hit.

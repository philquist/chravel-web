## A. Profile

- **Name:** Margaux Ellison, founder of a two-person bespoke travel atelier (Paris/NYC clientele)
- **Age range:** 38–45
- **Tech comfort:** High-intermediate. Lives in TravelJoy/Axus, Google Workspace, WhatsApp. Will not read docs; expects tools to be self-evident in 90 seconds.
- **Planning style:** Builds 30–60-item multi-city itineraries fast, then polishes a _client-facing deliverable_. Iterates 3–5 drafts per trip. Runs 6–10 concurrent client trips.
- **Main pain points:** re-keying confirmations from email/PDF; clients who never open the planning doc; ugly exports she has to redo in Canva; tools that look like consumer toys in front of $40k clients.
- **Budget sensitivity:** Low. Expenses $50–$150/mo tools without blinking — but only if the output is client-presentable. Polish IS the product she sells.
- **Why adopt Chravel:** Smart Import (Gmail/PDF/ICS ingestion with duplicate detection) + AI concierge that writes directly into a shared calendar + one link for the client party + PDF recap. That stack replaces 4 tools.
- **Why reject it:** Anything that says "Group Chat Travel App" on a client deliverable; per-trip free limits that gate her core workflow; AI confirm-cards that make batch building slower than typing into a spreadsheet; consumer-toned upsells in front of clients.

## B. Jobs-to-be-Done

1. When I am **assembling a 4-day Paris→Provence itinerary from a pile of confirmation emails**, I want to **bulk-import and have items land on the right days automatically**, so I can **build a draft in 30 minutes, not 3 hours**.
2. When I am **refining a draft**, I want to **tell an AI "move dinner to 8pm, add a Gordes detour Thursday" and have it apply cleanly**, so I can **iterate without re-keying**.
3. When I am **delivering to a client**, I want to **export a branded, elegant, correctly-timezoned PDF**, so I can **look like a luxury firm, not a group chat**.
4. When I am **sharing the live plan**, I want **clients to open one link and see a polished read-only itinerary without setup friction**, so I can **avoid babysitting their onboarding**.
5. When I am **running 8 client trips at once**, I want **per-trip workspaces with quiet notifications and no plan caps**, so I can **scale without noise or surprise paywalls**.

## E. Emotional Reaction

- **Impressed:** Smart Import scope (Gmail/PDF/ICS/screenshots with dedupe) `[OBSERVED — GT §6]`; invite preview polish; PDF table quality and clickable links; pending-action safety model _in principle_.
- **Confident:** during trip creation and invite-link generation — validation and copy are tight.
- **Confused:** dead "Export PDF" button in Itinerary view (did it work? is it me?); why Pro has no checkout; why her client "joined but can't see the trip" (GT §10.6).
- **Annoyed:** confirm-card-per-item during a 30-item build; 10-query AI cap mid-flow; one-file-at-a-time import; "The Group Chat Travel App" stamped on her client deliverable; US Letter + local-timezone times for a France itinerary.
- **Would abandon:** at the dead export button or the first PDF a client sees with consumer branding — both occur in week one. `[SIMULATED RISK]`
- **Would invite others:** clients yes (the preview link is shareable and attractive), peers no — she won't recommend it professionally until export is white-label and the broken surfaces are gone.

## F. Conversion Scores

- **Activation: 6/10.** Trip creation + calendar + places are fast and real; but the highest-value path (batch import → AI refine → export) hits three caps (10 queries, 1 export, single-file import) plus one dead button before the first deliverable ships.
- **Invite: 5/10.** The invite preview is genuinely strong `[OBSERVED — GT §5]`, but the account wall, zero-access guest role `[OBSERVED — GT §8]`, and the dashboard-missing-trip bug `[OBSERVED — GT §10.6]` mean her _clients_ — the people she invites — get the weakest experience.
- **Day-7 retention: 5/10.** The iterate→re-export loop is her daily loop and it's the most-gated, most-fragile path. If she upgrades to Explorer, retention jumps; if she hits the dead button first, she's gone.
- **Paid conversion: 6/10.** High willingness, obstructed path: Explorer is buyable and solves trips/exports; FC's marquee (voice) is broken `[OBSERVED — GT §10.2]`; Pro is `mailto:`-only `[OBSERVED — GT §7]`.
- **NPS: −10.** Promoter-grade feature vision, detractor-grade trust breaks (dead button, branding on deliverables, client-visible join bug).
- **Would they pay?** Yes — Explorer immediately ($9.99/mo is trivially expensable); FC ($19.99) the moment unlimited AI matters, which is week one. She'd pay **$49–99/mo happily** for a "Planner" tier: white-label PDF, trip templates, multi-file import, client read-only links.
- **Feature creating willingness to pay:** white-label/clean PDF export + unlimited AI queries. Export is the artifact she charges clients for.
- **Limit that triggers upgrade:** in order hit: 10 AI queries/user/trip (first build session) → 1 PDF export/trip (second draft) → 3 active trips (second client). All `[OBSERVED — GT §7; usePdfExportUsage.ts:5]`.
- **Natural vs annoying CTA:** Natural — GCal-sync gate and the export-limit prompt (she just experienced the value; "Unlimited PDF exports" on Explorer reads as exactly her problem `[OBSERVED — PlusUpsellModal.tsx:177]`). Annoying/premature — Pro paywall on the trip-type toggle before any value, and any upsell rendered while a client is in the shared trip `[SIMULATED RISK]`. Anti-CTA: `mailto:` for Pro is not a CTA at all.

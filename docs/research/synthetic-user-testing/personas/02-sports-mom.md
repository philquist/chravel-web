# Persona 2: Dana Whitfield — Mom Planning Youth Sports Travel

## A. Profile

- **Name:** Dana Whitfield
- **Age range:** 38–45
- **Tech comfort:** LOW-MODERATE. iPhone-only. Lives in iMessage, GroupMe, and a shared Google Sheet the team manager made in 2022. Has never heard of "Basecamp" as a feature name. Will not watch a tutorial.
- **Planning style:** Reactive and list-driven. She is the de facto "team mom" for her 12-year-old's travel soccer club: 10 other families, 3 games across 2 field complexes, one hotel block, one team dinner, snack duty, carpool seats.
- **Main pain points:** Game schedule changes Friday night; half the parents never see the update. Field addresses get pasted into chat 6 times. Nobody knows who paid for snacks. Carpool seat counts live in her head.
- **Budget sensitivity:** HIGH. She will not pay $9.99/mo for an app when GroupMe is free. A one-time fee for one tournament weekend is conceivable; a subscription is a hard sell.
- **Why she'd adopt:** One link that shows every parent the schedule, the hotel, and "what do I owe" — without anyone installing anything or learning anything.
- **Why she'd reject:** Any parent who clicks the invite link and hits a sign-up wall before seeing value; any flow with more than two steps; upgrade prompts before she's done one useful thing.

## B. Jobs-to-be-Done

1. When I am **handed the tournament schedule on Wednesday night**, I want to **get all 3 games with field addresses into one shared place in under 10 minutes**, so I can **stop re-texting the schedule every time it changes**.
2. When I am **rallying 10 other families**, I want to **send one link that works the first time, on their phones, with near-zero signup effort**, so I can **stop chasing individual parents who "never got it."**
3. When I am **organizing the Saturday team dinner**, I want to **poll parents on restaurant choice and headcount**, so I can **make a reservation once instead of negotiating in a 40-message thread**.
4. When I am **fronting money for snacks, gate fees, and the coach gift**, I want to **log what I paid and see who owes what**, so I can **get paid back without awkward reminder texts**.
5. When I am **standing at Field 7 at 7:40 AM**, I want to **open my phone and instantly see the next game time and address**, so I can **direct lost parents in one tap instead of scrolling chat history**.

## C. Full User Journey (all 16 steps)

### 1. Discovery / landing first impression

- **Tried:** Another team mom texts her "we used this for States." She opens chravelapp.com on her iPhone.
- **What code says happens:** Unauthenticated visitors get the marketing landing with pricing, FAQ, and sign-up modal [OBSERVED — ground-truth §3, `src/MarketingApp.tsx`, `src/components/landing/FullPageLanding.tsx`]. Pricing copy: "Start free. Upgrade when your trip gets serious." [OBSERVED — `PricingSection.tsx` per ground-truth §7].
- **Friction:** The product is framed as group _travel_; "AI concierge," "Pro tours," and "Events" are not her vocabulary. She is looking for "team schedule + group chat." [SIMULATED RISK]
- **Verdict:** Proceeds only because a trusted parent vouched. Cold discovery would likely lose her.

### 2. Sign-up / onboarding

- **Tried:** Signs up with her Gmail via Google OAuth.
- **What code says happens:** Email+password, Google, and Apple sign-in exist; email verification is required for email signups [OBSERVED — ground-truth §3]. Then an onboarding carousel: **10 screens** (`TOTAL_SCREENS = 10`: Welcome → Chat → Calendar → Concierge → Media → Payments → Places → Polls → Tasks → Final CTA), with a visible "Skip tour" link and an X button, ending in "Create Trip" / "Explore demo trip" CTAs [OBSERVED — `src/components/onboarding/OnboardingCarousel.tsx:37,50–114,247–253`].
- **Doc drift:** Ground truth says "9-screen carousel"; the file's own header comment says "Premium 5-screen product tour"; the code renders 10. [OBSERVED — drift across `OnboardingCarousel.tsx:2` vs `:37` vs ground-truth §3]
- **Friction:** 10 screens is 7 too many for her. She taps "Continue" twice, gets the gist ("chat + calendar + money"), and hits Skip. The skip affordance is real and prominent, so this is recoverable — but every screen between her and "Create Trip" is attrition for the _other_ 10 parents. [SIMULATED RISK]
- **Verdict:** Survives, mildly impatient.

### 3. Trip creation

- **Tried:** Creates "Memorial Day Classic — Springfield" for Fri–Sun.
- **What code says happens:** Single `CreateTripModal` with a **Trip Type selector first** (Consumer default / Pro / Event). Required: title (with duplicate-name validation), start/end dates, timezone; optional location, description, cover photo. Title placeholder: "e.g., Summer in Paris"; 80-char counter [OBSERVED — `src/components/CreateTripModal.tsx:416,479–510,561–597`].
- **The trap:** Pro trip categories include **"Sports"** with roster placeholder "e.g., Los Angeles Rams, Boys & Girls Club of Dallas" [OBSERVED — `CreateTripModal.tsx:528`]. A sports mom will plausibly tap "Sports" — and Pro creation is gated to Frequent Chraveler+ (beyond 1 free trial), surfacing an `UPGRADE_REQUIRED_PRO_TRIP` error toast with a "View Plans" action [OBSERVED — `CreateTripModal.tsx:308–313`, ground-truth §4, §7]. **A paywall inside trip creation, on her first session, because the category literally matched her use case.** [SIMULATED RISK — high-probability path]
- **Verdict:** If she stays on the default Consumer type, creation is genuinely simple (3 required fields). If she taps "Sports," there's real abandonment risk.

### 4. Inviting other parents

- **Tried:** Taps Invite, sends the link to the team GroupMe.
- **What code says happens:** Shareable `/join/{token}` link with copy/SMS/email/native share; optional expiration, max uses, require-approval toggle [OBSERVED — ground-truth §5]. The non-member parent who clicks it sees a **rich pre-auth preview**: cover image, trip name, destination, dates, "10 Chravelers already planning," "Trip starts in 3 days" urgency chip — before any signup [OBSERVED — `src/pages/JoinTrip.tsx:829–909`]. After auth, auto-join fires (the "P0 conversion path") and the invite code survives the OAuth round-trip via localStorage [OBSERVED — `JoinTrip.tsx:372–386,59,126–141`]. 7+ typed error states all have recovery CTAs [OBSERVED — `src/types/inviteErrors.ts`].
- **Friction — three real problems on this one screen:**
  1. **Leaked internal copy, verbatim:** unauthenticated invitees are shown _"Sign in with the standard Chravel dark auth flow to request access to this trip."_ and _"Google, Apple, and email sign-in remain available in the same dark modal."_ This is developer-facing spec text rendered to end users [OBSERVED — `JoinTrip.tsx:914–915,931–933`]. To a non-technical parent this reads as broken/unfinished software at the exact moment of maximum skepticism.
  2. **Unconditional "Request to Join" framing:** the join button says "Request to Join"/"Requesting..." and a blue **"Member Approval — A current trip member will review your request"** box renders whenever a logged-in user views the page (`!joining && user`) — _regardless of whether the invite actually requires approval_ [OBSERVED — `JoinTrip.tsx:937–971`; `invite.require_approval` is fetched at `:46` but never consulted for this UI]. Dana didn't enable approval; her parents are still told a human must review them. Several will assume they're "not in yet" and wait.
  3. **Full account required for any value:** `consumer_guest` has **NO access to any resource** — guests must become members [OBSERVED — ground-truth §8, `permissionMatrix.generated.ts`]. So the answer to the key invite question is: a parent gets a pretty preview card, then a mandatory account (plus email verification on the email path), or they get _nothing_ — no read-only schedule, ever.
- **Aftershock:** If approval _was_ on, there's a documented bug where an approved member's trip is **missing from their dashboard** after approval (trip_members.status column drift) [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 issue 6, DEBUG_PATTERNS.md]. For parents who will only click that link once, this is fatal: approved, notified, and still can't find the trip.
- **Verdict:** The preview and auto-join engineering is genuinely good; the copy bugs and false "approval" framing undercut it, and forced signup means realistically 6–7 of 10 families join, not 10.

### 5. Adding schedule items (3 games + hotel + dinner)

- **Friction:** Five-plus manual entries on a phone keyboard is the realistic path if she doesn't discover Smart Import; nothing in the trip UI obviously says "paste your tournament schedule PDF here." [SIMULATED RISK — discoverability]. Smart Import retries can duplicate shared trip data [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 issue 8, TEST_GAPS.md], and duplicated games on a shared schedule is precisely the chaos she came here to escape.
- **Verdict:** Workable; Smart Import could be magical but is undiscovered by this persona.

- **Friction:** 10 free queries is fine for a weekend; the confirm-card pattern ("Save to Trip?") is actually well-matched to a cautious user. Known design friction: model sometimes ignores its required JSON action format [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 design frictions], which to her looks like "the AI said it added it but nothing appeared." [SIMULATED RISK]
- **Verdict:** Pleasant surprise if it works; she'd use 3–4 queries total.

- **Friction:** "Basecamp" is mountaineering jargon; she'd never guess it means "our hotel." Saving 2 field complexes as places duplicates what she already typed into calendar events (place autocomplete) — two homes for the same address. [SIMULATED RISK]

### 8. Polls

- **Friction:** Minimal — this is one of the simplest surfaces in the app and maps 1:1 to her job. Votes only come from parents who actually completed signup (see step 4). A poll with 6 of 11 families voting doesn't settle the dinner debate. [SIMULATED RISK — downstream of invite funnel]
- **Verdict:** Best feature for her. Would screenshot results back to GroupMe for the holdouts.

- **Friction:** No checklist templates (she rebuilds "tournament packing list" from scratch each event) [SIMULATED RISK]. Assigning to the Hendersons only works if the Hendersons signed up — unassignable families remain her problem.
- **Verdict:** Adequate; works as a shared checklist.

### 10. Payments / splits

- **Tried:** Logs $84 snacks + $120 coach gift + $60 gate fees, split across 11 families; expects to send "you owe $24" requests.
- **What code says happens:** Log expense, equal/custom split, who-owes-whom summary, settle-up via **manual confirm or Venmo deeplink — no in-app money movement** [OBSERVED — ground-truth §6, §10 design frictions]. Free tier limit: `payments_basic` = **"3 splits/trip (Free)"** [OBSERVED — `src/billing/types.ts:30`]. Her three expenses are exactly at the cap — the 4th (Sunday lunch) hits the wall mid-weekend.
- **Enforcement gap:** `PaymentInput.tsx` contains **no entitlement/limit/upgrade check at all** (grep for limit|upgrade|tier|gate: zero matches) [OBSERVED — `src/components/payments/PaymentInput.tsx`]. Either the limit isn't enforced client-side (limit copy drift vs. billing config — quota theater, consistent with "storage quotas advisory-only," ground-truth §10 issue 7) or it fails somewhere deeper with no friendly upsell. [HYPOTHESIS — needs live test which behavior ships]
- **Documented hazards:** settlement mutation is not atomic — concurrent settle-ups can **double-credit**; idempotency keys missing on payment mutations so retries can duplicate [OBSERVED — ground-truth §10 issue 1, PLATFORM_AUDIT_CONSTITUTION.md]. Eleven parents settling on hotel Wi-Fi Sunday morning is the textbook trigger. Money errors are the single fastest trust-killer for this persona.
- **Dead-code landmine:** `src/components/payments/PaymentMessage.tsx` fabricates payment identifiers — "Mock payment identifiers for demo purposes," including a hardcoded fake Zelle number `'(555) 123-4567'` and a Venmo handle guessed from the payer's display name [OBSERVED — `PaymentMessage.tsx:38–50`]. No JSX usage found (`<PaymentMessage` has zero matches in `src/`) [OBSERVED — repo grep], so it appears unrendered today — but it's a live-wire if ever wired up: the app would tell a parent to Zelle a stranger's number.
- **Verdict:** Tracking works; settling is "Venmo with extra steps." The 3-split cap lands mid-trip with (at best) an awkward upsell and (at worst) silent non-enforcement.

### 13. Notifications

- **Tried:** Saturday: 11 parents × chat messages + poll votes + task updates.

- **Friction:** Parents' only escape from buzz-fatigue is killing push entirely at the OS level — and then they miss the Sunday 7 AM field change, the one notification that mattered.
- **Verdict:** Worst structural gap for a team-parent group.

### 14. Reviewing final itinerary

- **Tried:** Thursday night she wants one screen — or one printable page — with all games, addresses, hotel, dinner.
- **What code says happens:** Calendar list view serves the in-app need. **PDF export is gated to Explorer+** [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7]. - **Friction:** Sports parents print things and tape them to coolers. "Print the schedule" being a paid feature is the most viscerally annoying gate in her whole journey — it paywalls _leaving_ the app. 8 tabs is a lot of chrome for what she thinks of as "schedule + chat + money," though deep links from notifications mitigate it.
- **Verdict:** Functional in-app; the PDF gate is the moment she mutters "of course it costs money."

- **Verdict:** Returns if tournament #1 went well; the 3-trip cap is the first limit she _personally_ feels.

### 16. Pay-or-upgrade decision

- **Tried:** Weighs Explorer $9.99/mo.
- **What code says happens:** Upsell surfaces: `PlusUpsellModal` ("Start Free Trial," 14-day, "Maybe Later"), `TripPassModal` ("Annual Explorer pays for itself after ~3 trips") [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7]. - **Verdict:** The Trip Pass ($39.99/45 days) is the right-shaped product for her mental model ("pay for the season-ish, not forever") — though even that is ~2x what she'd impulse-pay for one weekend. The iOS dead-end means whatever willingness exists mostly evaporates in transit. [SIMULATED RISK]

## E. Emotional Reaction

- **Confident:** Creating the trip (default path), polls, tasks. These feel like the group text, but organized.
- **Confused:** "Basecamp" (jargon), "Request to Join" when no approval was set (are my parents in or not?), the "Sports" category paywall (am I supposed to pay because we're a team?), 8 tabs when she wanted 3.
- **Annoyed:** 10 onboarding screens; PDF export behind a paywall; the placeholder auth copy on the invite page ("…standard Chravel dark auth flow…") which reads as broken; notification firehose with no mute; "Subscribe on web" dead-end on her iPhone.
- **Impressed:** Invite link preview with the team's cover photo and "Trip starts in 3 days"; auto-join after sign-in; AI finding a pizza place that seats 25; who-owes-whom math.
- **Would abandon if:** fewer than ~8 of 10 families make it through signup (two-chat problem), OR any payment number is ever wrong (double-credit race), OR an approved parent can't find the trip on their dashboard.
- **Would invite others:** Yes — that's the whole point — but after the first tournament she'd preface the link with a paragraph of instructions ("you have to make an account, it says request but you're in automatically, ignore that"), which is itself the indictment.

## F. Conversion Scores

- **Activation: 6/10.** The organizer path (create → schedule → invite) is genuinely achievable in one evening if she stays on Consumer type. Deductions: 10-screen carousel, Sports-category paywall trap, Basecamp jargon.
- **Invite conversion: 4/10.** Best-in-class preview engineering sabotaged by mandatory accounts for zero-permission guests [OBSERVED — ground-truth §8], false "Member Approval" messaging, leaked dev copy, and (if approval is on) the missing-dashboard-trip bug [OBSERVED — §10.6]. The 10 less-savvy parents are the funnel, and the funnel leaks.
- **Day-7 retention: 5/10.** If the weekend goes well: photos + settle-up keep people returning Sunday–Tuesday. Threats: notification fatigue with no per-trip mute (uninstall driver), partial adoption pushing the real conversation back to GroupMe.
- **Paid conversion: 2/10.** HIGH budget sensitivity + the natural upgrade trigger (4th payment split) apparently unenforced in the creation UI [OBSERVED — PaymentInput grep; HYPOTHESIS on shipped behavior] + the iOS purchase dead-end ("Subscribe on web," no link-out) [OBSERVED — ground-truth §7].
